MS in ECE (Master of Science, Standard Program)

*All students who matriculated into the ECE MS program up to and including Spring 2020, please see the program requirements here.

The MS in ECE is a three-semester program comprised of 97 units of graduate coursework (600-level and above) available at the Pittsburgh and CMU–Africa campuses. The Standard program has the following requirements:

  • 60 units of ECE Core courses
  • 24 units of CIT Elective courses
  • 12 units of General Technical Elective courses
  • 1 unit of Introduction to Graduate Studies

Up to 27 units of research project coursework can be used to satisfy program requirements. Students interested in conducting additional project work may pursue the Intensive Project Option outlined below. All students apply for research projects through the Student Project Tracker site.

Up to 12 units of undergraduate coursework (300-level or higher) can qualify to be substituted toward the 96 units of Core or Elective requirements. Qualifying coursework must be offered by the same department as either an approved Core or Elective course.

Intensive Project Option requirements

The Intensive Project Option provides students the option to work intensively on a single research project with a CMU faculty member. A student will take a total of 36 units of graduate-level research to count towards their degree in two separate phases. The first phase of the Intensive Project Option is 12 units that will be taken no sooner than the student’s second semester in the program. The second phase is 24 units that will be taken in the following semester. These 36 units satisfy the degree requirements as follows:
  • 60 units of ECE Core (36 units of Core coursework + 24 units of Intensive Research)
  • 24 units of CIT Elective (12 units of CIT Elective coursework + 12 units of Intensive Research)
  • 12 units of General Technical Elective courses
  • 1 unit of Introduction to Graduate Studies

D. Introduction to Graduate Studies

1 unit of Introduction to Graduate Studies (18-989) must be taken.

This course must be completed in your first semester. Conflicts are not permitted.

E. MS Graduate Project Coursework (Standard Program)

Students may count up to 27 project units toward degree requirements. 12 project units can count toward Core requirements and up to 15 project units can count toward CIT Elective requirements. Only 15 units of MS Graduate Project can be taken in any given semester unless a student is pursuing the Intensive Project Option.

For the Intensive Project Option, students will register for 12 units of 18-980 no sooner than their second semester in the program. Students will then register for the remaining 24 units of 18-982 in the following semester.

Explore the Student Project Tracker (SPT) Overview for Students page to learn about research project eligibility requirements, compensation options, finding and applying for projects, and more. 

Students can use the Student Project Tracker (SPT) website to apply for a project and work with their primary advisor for course approval and registration.

Course Restrictions Outside the College of Engineering

ECE master’s students in the Standard program will take 97 units for their degree, 85 units of which must be from within the College of Engineering (61 from within ECE, 24 from within CIT; see above). This rule applies to all students matriculating Fall 2020 or later and to all IMB students who sign their intent form after the start of the Fall 2020 semester. IMB students will be beholden to the program requirements in place when they sign their intent form and officially agree to enter the program.

*Please note: Additional units can be taken beyond the required 97 units described above in order to diversify your course schedule; however, they will not be counted toward the program requirements. Students who wish to take more than the recommended 36 units per semester (after their first semester) should make an appointment with their academic advisor to review their planned schedule.

Note on Prerequisites, Antirequisites, and Crosslists

Prerequisites

Students can see course prerequisites in their SIO and Stellic accounts. Prerequisites are typically not enforced for graduate students during registration, but it is a student's responsibility to review this information and to contact course instructors if there is any question about having sufficient preparation or background. If a prerequisite is enforced for a specific course, this information is available in the course "Notes" visible in SIO.

Some courses will list "prerequisite knowledge" as opposted to a specific prerequisite course number (e.g., knowledge of a certain programming language). This information is also visible in SIO.

Anti-Requisites

Antirequisites represent courses whose content overlaps significantly or entirely. Registration may or may not be barred in the registration system, but a student may NOT count more than one course sharing an antirequisite relationship toward their degree. Course offerings across the university are extremely dynamic, so antirequisite lists may not be exhaustive. It is a student's responsibility to avoid taking multiple courses whose content overlaps, or else risk losing credit for the redundant course(s). If a student has doubts over which courses are considered overlapping, the student should speak with their academic advisor and submit a petition if there is a possibility that the courses overlap.

Crosslisted Courses

Sometimes multiple course numbers are crosslisted with one another, meaning they are taught at the same place and time. If more than one ECE course number is crosslisted, expectations will differ depending on which course number a student is registered for. A student may not count more than one of a set of crosslisted course numbers to their degree.

Maximum Units Allowed

Students pursuing the MS Standard degree cannot register for their final semester if they have already completed 120 units of coursework. These units include courses taken for audit, pass/no pass, and withdrawal.
